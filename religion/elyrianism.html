Introduction

Elyrianism is a Divonian dualistic gnostic deistic monolatric religion based on the life, teachings, and miracles of Elyria. The religion itself is split into two main branches and is internally very diverse. It has been spread across the world through religious missions, crusades, and trade. The religion started out as a minor string of unified religions in the fringes of the Athyrian Empire and grew to become the sole religion of the Empire and eventually the most dominant faith in Amur. The religion centres around the idea of gnosis, secret knowledge, which they deemed necessary to achieve salvation.

Elyrianism grew out of Divonianism, the native monotheistic religion in the region of Divonia, beginning as a sect of the religion believing in a dithetic religion, elevating the lesser evil being of the religion into godhood. Eventually, an adherent of the faith, Elyria, said to be the second avatar of the Archon Alyra, spread her own theology based on those practices. By the end of her life, she had such a great following that it had let the other native religions of Divonia into extinction. From there it saw itself grow past the region and into many parts of the Athyrian Empire. After two centuries of intermittent and localised persecution by Athyrian authorities, the Emperor Elyrianos declared the faith the official and sole religion of the Empire. At that point the religion grew to encompass the entire Empire and spread thenceforth by trade, warfare, and religious missions.
